Here are some links and short descriptions to recommendations on picking a parts for your PC.

Motherboard Recommendations

Your motherboard decision should be based on what type of PC you want to build. Each brand and type of motherboard supports different things. If you care more about future proofing, you may want to choose a motherboard that has current or next generation hardware meaning it will likely support future parts like RAM and CPU’s. If you care only about a decent PC for the time being, you may be able to go a bit cheaper on a motherboard for current generation parts. Different motherboards also support different speeds in parts such as storage devices that are clocked at different speeds. Refer to this link when choosing a motherboard. Click Here!

Case Recommendations

Cases are pretty much just aesthetic with respect to motherboard size and airflow. That being said, your choice on a case should be based on those three things. Choose a case that you think looks good, but read reviews to see how well it does with airflow. When you decide on a motherboard, you will be able to choose on a case based on whether or not it supports ATX or Micro-ATX sized motherboards. Refer to this link for a better reference at picking a case: Click Here!

CPU Recommendations

CPU’s or “computer processing units” are the brain of the computer. There are pretty much two main brands to go with, Intel and AMD. Your choice in processor will be based on what you plan on doing with the computer. If you plan on doing a lot of multi-tasking, you may want a CPU with a few more cores. If you plan on doing strictly gaming, a faster processor with less cores may be necessary. Refer to this link that goes more in depth about what CPU to choose for multiple occasions: Click Here!

RAM Recommendations

The two best things to consider when choosing RAM is speed and the amount. Pretty much the standard in terms of amount of RAM these days is 16gb for most applications. It’s enough to game with, multitask and then anything else you may want to do with your PC. Although, you can get by with 8gb, and then you can get as much as a few hundred GB if you have the money to spend on it. Refer to this link when choosing RAM: Click Here!

GPU Recommendations

There are a ton of factors to consider when choosing a GPU. Sometimes you may not even need a GPU, and then other times if you plan on doing a lot of gaming, a strong GPU is needed. GPU’s can also vary a lot in price so there’s that to consider. Refer to this video that goes very in to depth on GPU’s: Click Here!

Storage Recommendations

The two biggest things to consider for storage are size and speed. It’s always a good idea to have a lot of storage on your computer, and buying Hard Drives these days is relatively inexpensive. But when it comes to speed, you will often want your storage for things like your operating system to be fast and efficient which is when a SSD would come in handy. Refer to this video for a storage buying guide: Click Here!

PSU Recommendations

The power supply you choose for your PC will be based on power wattage and power standards. There are different tiers to efficiency of power supplies which correlates to how efficiently the power supply draws power from the wall. The wattage is how much power the PSU supplies to the system. This is also a case where more than enough wattage is a good thing, but not necessary. Refer to this link as a more in depth guide: Click Here!

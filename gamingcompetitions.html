CSGO

Counter Strike Global Offensive is one of the longest standing esports whose player is still active. Most CS-GO tournaments are online and you can find information on events with open registration here. Some registration events require a team, and some can be registered for solo.

League of Legends

League of Legends has long been one of, if not the most popular esport in the world. Commonly the most viewed game on twitch, the game has a huge playerbase and a large competitive scene as well. To find tournaments to enter with a team or solo, check out this website.

Dota 2

Dota 2 is one of the few MOBAs outside of League of Legends to have a thriving community well after it's release date. It has a very dedicated community as most e-sports which start as community mods do. You can learn more here about how you can get invovled in the competitive scene.

Fortnite

Fortnite took the Battle-Royal genre by storm when it released in 2017, and remains the most active Battle-Royal all of these years later. Fortnite has likely the most active developers of any e-sport and is constantly getting new content. You can find out about Fortnite tournaments from their website.

Overwatch

The First-Person arena shooter esports scene is quite contested right now. With Overwatch and Valorant both competeing with CS-GO hoping for that top spot. With Overwatch 2 just around the corner the competitive scene is soon to be at its most active. You can find more about future Overwatch events here.

Valorant

Valorant is Riot-Games take on the First-Person arena shooter. It took the scene by storm when it released offering a mix between the pure FPS style of CS-GO and the Hero-Shooter style of Overwatch. Riot-Games has a lot of experience with competitive communities being the group behind League of Legends. To find out more about how you can enter upcoming events you can visit here.

Super Smash Brothers Melee

Super Smash Bro's Melee can now be played entirely on your PC. Thanks to a moddified dolphin emulator created by a community member known as Fizzi you can now play Melee online with lightning fast roll-back netcode. Thanks to this the community has been able to host online tournaments and has thrive over the last few years. You can find more information on Slippi here. And you can learn more about upcomming tournaments here.
